Frequently Asked Questions

80% of fire related deaths occur at night when people may be sleeping and when situational awareness is at its lowest and response time is at it longest. It is a bit easier for parents to orient and respond but for children, disabled and the older population it may take 2-3 times longer to even react to the first alarm and that critical time may mean the difference in being able to get out of the home in time versus being trapped.


Nearly 50% of home fire fatalities occur in homes with working smoke detectors. The only thing that smoke alarms do is alert you in presence of smoke. While having smoke detectors in your home can certainly increase your response time with a fire, they in no way guarantee your family’s safety or ability for everyone to get out in time. Although smoke alarms are a vital part in your home’s fire safety plan, year after year people continue to die due to the fact that they were unable to get out of the home in time even in homes with working smoke detectors.
